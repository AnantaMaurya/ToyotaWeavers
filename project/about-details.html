01 Natural Materials

Our Fibre Portfolio

Toyota Weavers offers quality without compromise - whether you seek luxury appeal, sustainable choices, or regional exclusivity, our portfolio ensures every product reflects craftsmanship, comfort, and responsibility.

Natural Cellulosic Fibre

Cotton

100% Cotton offers natural softness, breathability, and everyday comfort. Supima Cotton is premium American extra-long staple cotton known for exceptional softness, strength, and color retention. Egyptian Cotton delivers luxurious feel, fine fibres, and superior durability. Organic Cotton is grown without harmful chemicals, supporting cleaner farming and conscious sourcing. Recycled Cotton transforms pre- or post-consumer textile waste into new fibre, reducing landfill impact and resource use. Together, these cotton choices meet performance, luxury, and sustainability demands.

Structural Plant Fibre

European Flax Linen

European Flax linen is a premium natural fibre known for its strength, breathability, and timeless appeal. It requires relatively low water and fewer agricultural inputs, making it a considered choice for sustainable production. Naturally moisture-wicking and temperature-regulating, linen becomes softer with use while maintaining its durability. Well suited for table linens, upholstery, and lightweight bedding, it offers lasting comfort with a refined finish.

Golden Fibre

Jute

Jute is a durable natural fibre widely used for its strength and versatility. Grown primarily in alluvial regions, it matures quickly and requires relatively low agricultural inputs. Its coarse texture and natural finish make it suitable for bags, rugs, mats, carpets, and upholstery backing. Biodegradable and renewable, jute serves as a practical material for applications where durability and environmental consideration are important.

Sustainable Stalk Fibre

Hemp

Hemp is a durable natural fibre valued for its strength and low environmental impact. It grows quickly with minimal water and inputs. Naturally breathable and long-lasting, hemp softens with use while retaining structure, making it suitable for upholstery, outdoor textiles, and workwear.

Regenerated Cellulose Fibre

Tencel

Tencel, a branded lyocell fibre, is sourced sustainably from eucalyptus wood pulp through an award-winning closed-loop process. It delivers outstanding moisture management, silky softness, and strength comparable to synthetic alternatives. Ideal for sensitive skin, Tencel is used in our premium bedding and apparel lines. The solvent-spinning method recycles nearly all water and chemicals, making it one of the most environmentally responsible fibres available.

Semi-Synthetic Fibre

Modal

Modal is a premium cellulosic fibre extracted from beech tree pulp, known for exceptional softness and colour retention. It resists shrinkage and fading while remaining breathable and smooth. We incorporate modal into luxury apparel and bedding collections where comfort and longevity matter. Produced through a closed-loop process, it represents a more sustainable alternative to conventional viscose, minimising waste and environmental impact.

Semi-Synthetic Fibre

Viscose

Viscose is a semi-synthetic fibre derived from wood pulp, offering a silk-like feel at an accessible price. It drapes beautifully, absorbs moisture well, and breathes comfortably against the skin. At Toyota Weavers, we use viscose for apparel and soft furnishings where fluidity and lustre are desired. Though plant-based, it undergoes chemical processing, so we source from responsible manufacturers prioritising reduced emissions and water recovery.

Regenerated Cellulose Fibre

Rayon

Rayon is a versatile regenerated cellulosic fibre made from wood and agricultural by-products. It mimics the comfort of natural fibres while allowing diverse textures from crisp to velvety. At Toyota Weavers, we employ rayon in apparel and home textiles requiring excellent drape and absorbency. As a plant-based material, it offers a renewable foundation, and we prioritise suppliers committed to responsible forestry and cleaner production methods.

Waste-to-Wealth Fibre

Banana

Banana fibre is extracted from the pseudo-stem of banana plants, a by-product of fruit cultivation. Strong and biodegradable, it offers a coarse yet versatile texture suitable for ropes, mats, and decorative textiles. At Toyota Weavers, we value banana fibre for its sustainability credentials and unique character. It requires minimal additional land or water, turning agricultural waste into durable, eco-conscious material.

Specialty Natural Fibres

Bamboo & Coir

These specialty fibres offer unique properties and sustainable sourcing. Bamboo is soft and breathable. Coir, sourced from coconut husks, is durable and water-resistant. Together, they support diverse applications from apparel to home textiles.

04 Quality Metric

Thread Count & Durability

Higher thread count means finer yarns and tighter weaves, but quality matters more than numbers.

Exceptional Quality

Our fabrics combine high thread counts with premium fibres for superior softness, strength, and longevity that endures wash after wash.

What It Means

Thread count is the total number of horizontal (weft) and vertical (warp) threads woven into a single square inch of fabric. We believe that true quality is found where precision meets performance.

Our Standards

  • 200-400 TC: Crisp Percale
  • 400-600 TC: Twill Weave
  • 600-1200: Smooth Sateen
  • Jacquard: Custom counts
